Disney World Trip

Our Trip to Disney World!

Planning

Planning a trip for Disney World may have been the most difficult part of the trip itself. My wife and I planned for almost a year for what parks we would visit and where we would eat. The process was a little more grueling than it should have been.

Without ever visiting the park you have to pick where you are going to eat and what rides you wanna ride at very specific times. You have to plan this and submit it months in advance to reserve your placement or take the risk of not being able to enjoy all the experiences the parks have to offer. For about 300,000¢ I thought it would have been easier to plan.

Travel

This is just a little tidbit of my opinions of how someone should plan to travel. My wife and I drove from St. Louis to Orlando. The trip by mapquest should have taken just a little under 18 hours. But when you have multiple people, one being a 2 year old, there is no way to run it through with no stops in between. I made the drive overnight myself, with about 5-6 stops accounting for 19-20 hours of travel. That being said getting there was not as bad as you think, even with the few meltdowns from my daughter who was to be honest, an angel for the long distance we traveled.

Coming back home after a week long trip on the other hand, was just awful. I, myself was just so tired and a little crabby during the car ride. My daughter realizing we were leaving "The Happiest Place on Earth" was getting upset a lot more cause she wanted to be out of her car seat and back at the parks. And my wife was tired as well from the long nights and early days so we could get as much as we could out of our trip. I couldn't make the whole drive back home by myself for fear of falling asleep. I will never make the drive to Disney again. That isn't to say we won't be going back, but I think a flight will definitely be in our future.

Disney World

Now the part that everyone actually cares about. Disney World!!! Did we like it, how was it, were the rides fun, ect. Well, all in all, I would give the trip 8 out of 10. My daughter had so many instances of just being in awe, that it made the trip worth it on it's own. She showed so much emotion meeting Elsa(Frozen) in person. You could see how nervous, and excited she was all at the same time. It was amazing to see a little girl get so excited to meet one of her favorite princesses. And that was just one of the many events that we enjoyed. I would say that the events and her reactions were priceless on their own. Without spoiling anything for anyone else the only reason Disney only had an 8 was due to the massive crowds and eternal wait times if you didn't have a fast pass for the rides you were wanting to enjoy.
